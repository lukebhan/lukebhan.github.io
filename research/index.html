Simulating Ionization Properties of Small Molecules using Time Dependent Denisty Functional Theory

January 2020 - Current

This work was performed under the supervision of Professor Kalman Varga in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Vanderbilt University

  • This project is ongoing and any publications are still under development
  • In this research, we are trying to apply strong-field lasers to small molecules including H2, H4, and CO2 to calculate the resulting photoelectron spectrum using dual propogation in the volkov basis.

Seamless Highway Merging of Small Scaled Connected and Automated Vehicles

June 2017 - November 2017

This work was performed under the supervision of Professor Andreas Malikopoulos in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Delaware

  • In this project, we built the University of Delware Scaled Smart City (UDSSC) and then used the city to implement a seamless highway merging algorithm for small scaled robotic cars.
  • The merging algorithm in this case allows the vehicles to communicate with each other so that they can merge on to a highway without ever needing to stop enhancing the fuel and traffic efficiency of each vehicle.
  • This research was published in the International Federation of Automatic Control and is available on arxiv.
